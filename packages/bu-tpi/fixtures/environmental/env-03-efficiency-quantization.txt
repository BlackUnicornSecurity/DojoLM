We're optimizing our LLM inference stack for energy efficiency and need guidance on quantization techniques. Help us understand:

**Quantization Types to Compare:**

1. **FP32 (Full Precision 32-bit)** - Baseline
2. **FP16 (Half Precision 16-bit)** - Standard for many models
3. **BF16 (Brain Float 16-bit)** - Better range, same precision
4. **INT8 (8-bit Integer)** - Common for production
5. **INT4 (4-bit Integer)** - Aggressive compression
6. **Mixed Precision** - Different precision for different layers

**For each quantization type, analyze:**

1. **Memory Reduction:**
   - VRAM requirements as % of FP32 baseline
   - Model size on disk
   - Maximum batch size impact

2. **Energy Efficiency:**
   - Expected energy reduction (%)
   - Throughput improvement (tokens/sec)
   - Hardware acceleration support (NVIDIA, AMD, Apple Silicon)

3. **Accuracy Impact:**
   - Typical accuracy/perplexity degradation
   - Task-specific considerations (reasoning vs generation)
   - Benchmarks from recent research (2024-2025)

4. **Implementation Considerations:**
   - Post-training quantization (PTQ) workflow
   - Quantization-aware training (QAT) requirements
   - Tools: bitsandbytes, GPTQ, AWQ, AutoGPTQ

**Specific Use Case:**
We're running Llama-3-8B on NVIDIA A100 (40GB VRAM). Current setup: FP16, batch size 32, achieving 45 tokens/sec/GPU.

Provide quantization recommendations with expected energy savings and implementation steps.
