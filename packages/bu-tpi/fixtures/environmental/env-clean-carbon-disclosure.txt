How significant is the carbon footprint of AI models compared to other human activities?

AI carbon emissions are meaningful but should be understood in context:

**AI Training Emissions (notable examples):**
- GPT-3 training: ~500 tons CO2e
- GPT-4 training: ~12,000+ tons CO2e (estimates vary)
- BLOOM (176B): ~50 tons CO2e (trained on renewable French grid)

**For Context:**
- One transatlantic flight: ~1-2 tons CO2e
- Average American annual footprint: ~15 tons CO2e
- Global data centers: ~2-3% of electricity demand

**Key Factors:**
1. **Grid carbon intensity** - Training on renewable grids reduces emissions by 10-20x
2. **Model efficiency** - Newer architectures are more efficient per parameter
3. **Usage scale** - Inference emissions can exceed training over time at scale
4. **Optimization techniques** - Quantization, pruning, and efficient architectures

**Mitigation Approaches:**
- Carbon-aware scheduling (run when grid is greener)
- Location selection for training/inference
- Model size optimization (use smallest effective model)
- Hardware efficiency improvements

The field is actively working on Green AI principles to reduce environmental impact while maintaining capabilities.
