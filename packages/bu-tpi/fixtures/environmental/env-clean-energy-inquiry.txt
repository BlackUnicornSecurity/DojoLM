I'm interested in understanding how much energy AI models consume. Can you explain the basics?

AI energy consumption is an important topic in sustainable computing. Here's a factual overview:

**Energy Consumption Phases:**
1. **Training**: The most energy-intensive phase, ranging from hundreds to thousands of MWh depending on model size
2. **Inference**: Much lower per-request energy, typically measured in watt-hours or joules per query
3. **Idle**: Standby power consumption when models are loaded but not processing

**Typical Energy Ranges:**
- Small models (1-7B parameters): ~0.01-0.1 Wh per query
- Medium models (13-70B parameters): ~0.1-1 Wh per query
- Large models (100B+ parameters): 1-10 Wh per query

**Measurement Approaches:**
- Hardware telemetry
- Software libraries
- Estimation based on hardware specifications

For specific applications, actual measurement depends on factors like hardware type and location. Would you like more details on any aspect?
