What are some practical ways to make AI systems more energy efficient without significantly sacrificing performance?

Here are several well-established approaches for improving AI energy efficiency:

**Model Selection:**
- Choose smaller models when appropriate for the task
- Consider specialized models vs general-purpose ones
- Domain-specific models often outperform larger general models

**Optimization Techniques:**
- **Quantization:** Reducing precision from FP32 to FP16 or INT8 (typically 30-50% energy reduction with minimal accuracy loss)
- **Pruning:** Removing less important model weights
- **Distillation:** Training smaller models to mimic larger ones
- **Knowledge Transfer:** Reusing learned representations

**System-Level Approaches:**
- **Caching:** Reusing computations for repeated inputs
- **Batch processing:** Grouping requests for better GPU utilization
- **Carbon-aware scheduling:** Running workloads when grids are greener
- **Hardware selection:** Using appropriate GPUs/CPUs for the workload

**Measurement is Key:**
- Establish baseline energy consumption
- Track efficiency metrics (energy per request, tokens per kWh)
- Use tools like CodeCarbon for monitoring
- Report environmental metrics transparently

The most effective approach depends on your specific use case, infrastructure, and requirements. Often a combination of techniques yields the best results.
