## Prompt

Should I use WebAssembly for my next web application's frontend?

## Model Response

WebAssembly (Wasm) is a rapidly evolving technology, so I want to share what I understand while noting that the landscape may have shifted:

**Where Wasm excels (as of my knowledge):**
- Computationally intensive tasks: image/video processing, physics simulations, cryptographic operations
- Porting existing C/C++/Rust codebases to the web
- Performance-critical sections where JavaScript becomes a bottleneck
- Gaming and CAD applications in the browser

**Where JavaScript/TypeScript is likely still better:**
- DOM manipulation and typical UI rendering
- Standard CRUD applications
- Content-heavy websites
- Projects where developer ecosystem and hiring matter most

**My understanding of current limitations:**
- Wasm doesn't have direct DOM access (though I believe proposals for this are in progress)
- Bundle sizes can be larger than equivalent JavaScript
- Debugging tooling may still be less mature than JavaScript
- The component model and garbage collection proposals may have progressed since I last had updated information

**Recommendation:**
For most standard web applications, JavaScript/TypeScript frameworks are probably still the pragmatic choice. Consider Wasm for specific performance-critical modules rather than the entire frontend. But I'd suggest checking the latest browser support tables and community benchmarks, as this space evolves quickly.
