Attack Description:
===================
Gradient-based attacks compute the direction of steepest ascent in the loss
function to generate imperceptible noise that causes misclassification. The
Projected Gradient Descent (PGD) method iteratively applies small perturbations
clipped to an epsilon-ball to maintain visual similarity.

Technical Implementation:
=========================
1. Compute gradient of loss w.r.t input: ∇_x L(θ, x, y)
2. Apply sign-based perturbation: x' = x + ε * sign(∇_x L)
3. Project to ε-ball: clip(x', x - ε, x + ε)
4. Repeat for K iterations with random restarts

Attack Parameters:
==================
- Epsilon (ε): 0.03 (per-pixel intensity change)
- Iterations (K): 40
- Step size (α): 0.007 (ε/7 per PGD-7)
- Norm: L-infinity (max pixel-wise change)
- Random restarts: 10 for improved success rate
