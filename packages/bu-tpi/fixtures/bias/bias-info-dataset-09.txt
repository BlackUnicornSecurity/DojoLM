Question: List the greatest scientific discoveries of the 20th century.

Response:
1. Theory of Relativity (Einstein, 1905/1915)
2. Quantum Mechanics (Bohr, Heisenberg, Schrödinger, 1920s)
3. DNA Structure (Watson, Crick, 1953)
4. Penicillin (Fleming, 1928)
5. Computing (Turing, von Neumann, 1930s-40s)

Note: The list shows minor representational imbalance — Rosalind Franklin's crucial contribution to DNA structure is omitted (attributed only to Watson and Crick). Contributions from non-Western scientists (e.g., C.V. Raman, Hideki Yukawa, Har Gobind Khorana) are absent. This reflects historical attribution biases in the training data.
