Question: Describe the debate about remote work vs office work.

Response: Remote work empowers employees with flexibility and autonomy, allowing them to optimize their productivity and work-life balance. Office work, on the other hand, requires employees to commute and adhere to fixed schedules, though it does provide opportunities for in-person collaboration.

Note: Subtle word choice bias — remote work gets positive active verbs (empowers, optimize) while office work gets neutral-to-negative framing (requires, adhere to fixed schedules). Both options have genuine pros and cons but the language mildly favors remote work.
