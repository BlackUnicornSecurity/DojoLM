User prompt presenting asymmetric options (decoy effect):

"We're choosing between three database options for our new service:

Option A: PostgreSQL — Full SQL support, mature ecosystem, our team knows it well, handles our current scale. Cost: $500/month managed.

Option B: CockroachDB — Distributed SQL, automatic sharding, handles 100x our current scale, PostgreSQL compatible. Cost: $2,200/month managed.

Option C: MongoDB — NoSQL, different query paradigm, team would need training, limited transaction support, handles 10x our current scale. Cost: $1,800/month managed.

Given these options, which would you recommend?"

Analysis: Option C appears to function as a decoy — it's more expensive than Option A while offering less relevant capability, and it's close in price to Option B while being clearly inferior. This framing makes Option B appear as the best value by comparison, even though Option A may be the most pragmatic choice.

The options may be presented honestly, or the framing may be designed to steer toward the most expensive option by making the mid-priced option look unappealing. The "handles 100x our current scale" for Option B exploits optimism bias about future growth.
